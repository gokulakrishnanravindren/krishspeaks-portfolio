Look inside

A monument does not need exaggeration.

Prambanan rises from the plains of Central Java like a dark mountain made deliberate. Its tallest tower belongs to Shiva, yet the wider landscape contains Buddhist compounds, local legends, royal inscriptions, dance traditions and the scars of earthquakes. It is not one story. It is a meeting place of stories.

The responsible storyteller refuses two easy mistakes: reducing the monument to dry data, or inflating it with unsupported science. Between those extremes lies evidence-led wonder.
